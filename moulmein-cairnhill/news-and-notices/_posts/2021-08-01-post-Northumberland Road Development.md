<p><strong>UPCOMING RESIDENTIAL DEVELOPMENT AT NORTHUMBERLAND ROAD</strong>
</p>
<p>We wish to inform you of an upcoming residential development at Northumberland
Road. This residential development would comprise of 3 blocks of 23-storey
apartments (estimated 407 units) with multi-storey and basement car parks,
communal facilities, childcare centre and shops.</p>
<p>As part of the development plan for the site, the following infrastructure
works would be carried out:</p>
<p>• Removal of existing surface carpark.</p>
<p>• New road from Race Course Road into the development entrance.</p>
<p>• New footpath along the development boundary along Race Course Road/
Gloucester Road.</p>
<p>• Removal of existing linkway connecting Farrer Park View to the Farrer
Park MRT Exit E. A temporary, lighted footpath will be constructed to connect
Farrer Park View to Farrer Park MRT Exit E via Gloucester Road. This will
be removed once the permanent footpath is ready for use.</p>
<p>• New through-block linkway, open 24/7 for public access, will be constructed
to connect Farrer Park View to Farrer Park MRT Exit E.</p>
<p>Removal of Car Park</p>
<p>The existing car park will cease to operate on 2 August 2021. Motorists
can find alternative parking spaces at nearby public parking spaces such
as Connexion, Hilton Garden Inn.</p>
<p>The project will adopt a Prefabricated Prefinished Volumetric Construction
(PPVC) method, which involves the manufacturing of modules (complete with
internal finishes, fixtures and fittings) in factories. The completed modules
will then be transported to the site for installation. This will reduce
the amount of construction activities on-site, improve site safety and
achieve higher productivity and quality.</p>
<p>The developer will work with the authorities to ensure that the development
is sensitively designed to take into consideration the neighbouring developments.
During the construction period, we will erect information boards and directional
signs to keep the public informed of changes in pedestrian circulation
network for the area.</p>
<p>The developer will endeavour to minimise disturbances to the neighbourhood.
We seek your kind understanding on any inconvenience caused during the
construction period.</p>
