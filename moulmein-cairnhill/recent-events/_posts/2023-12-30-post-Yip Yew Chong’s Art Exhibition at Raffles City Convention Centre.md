<p>Yip Yew Chong’s art is inspiring. You dwell on the intricate detail of
his paintings, which draw you back in time to a familiar place in our special
little island.</p>
<p>His 60m painting depicting 27 adjoining pieces of art about different
scenes of Singapore, is nothing short of special. Three pieces of this
masterpiece captivated me:</p>
<p>1. Kreta Ayer, where I started my volunteer journey 20 years ago. It’s
also where Yew Chong grew up.</p>
<p>2.Little India, where I now serve and am familiar with the many sights
and sounds.</p>
<p>3.Toa Payoh Central, where I grew up playing in the park, around the fountain
in front of the library, and riding the mini electric cars around the fountain,
paying 50cents to the old Uncle who would set up these mini electric cars
in the evenings.</p>
<p>Congratulations Yew Chong, for captivating scores of Singaporeans and
friends of Singapore. My MoCa residents and I thoroughly enjoyed the experience
today!</p>
